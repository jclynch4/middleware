Origins of Cuban People

    HOME   NATURE   PEOPLE  

Cuba was colonized by the Spaniards at the beginning of the 15th century, when the island was inhabited by 100,000 Indians.

With the arrival of the Spaniards to the native populations of the Caribbean, the blacks of the African colonies located in this region of the world were introduced. There is a great ethnic mix of the Cuban population, basically composed of Caucasians, African blacks and mulattoes; the latter is the result of the mixture of the first two population groups and, to a lesser extent, of other populations such as China and Indo-American.

Cuba: a society always committed to its history.

Cuba have an ethnic mix composed mainly of their European genes (72%), while 20% come from Africans and 8% from the ancient Native Americans.
