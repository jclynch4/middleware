Cubans are sociable and friendly people. They can converse with anyone and seem to spend most of the time interacting with others. As you walk through the streets, you will see people sitting outside their homes talking to their neighbors. It is common to see couples kissing, children kicking soccer balls and men playing chess. People are intimately interconnected, which is especially refreshing for foreigners who come from countries where technology has replaced interpersonal communication. In fact, the lack of technology in Cuba (cell phones, televisions, and computers) has made socialization a necessity.

Cuba has an average population density of

105 inhabitants per km2. Despite the massive

immigration of Cubans abroad,the population

of the island continues to grow every year.
